About

The National Conference of Environmental Doctoral Students on New Theories and New Technology of Environmental Science and Engineering (“Environmental Conference for Doctoral Students”), is held by Tsinghua University, Chinese Society for Environmental Sciences (Environmental Education Committee), Collaborative Innovation Center for Regional Environmental Quality, State Key Joint Laboratory of Environment Simulation and Pollution Control, sponsored by Xi’an University of Architecture and Technology and Frontiers of Environmental Science & Engineering(Journal). Adhering to the purpose “exchange and idea-collision towards a road of innovation, widely accumulate into remarkable words,” the conference is themed “New Theories and New Technology of Environmental Science and Engineering”. It has actively been serving as a high-level academic platform for students from environmental and related disciplines.

The annually held conference has so far witnessed 11 successful sessions. A total of 1913 abstracts were received, with a total of 1092 participants and 22 guests (including more than 10 academicians including Academician Qian Yi, Academician Hao Jiming, Academician Yang Zhifeng, Academician He Kebin, Academician Qu Jiuhui and etc.) Doctoral students from more than 100 domestic and foreign universities, including nearly half of the first-class universities and almost all universities with environmental science and engineering disciplines, covering over 30 related majors such as environmental science, environmental engineering, chemical engineering, municipal engineering, energy engineering, economic management, mechanical engineering, bioengineering, ecological engineering, nuclear science and technology. With the increasing popularity and influence, the “Environmental Conference for Doctoral Students” has been widely recognized by teachers and doctoral students from environmental field in China, and has gradually become the most influential platform of academic exchange for doctoral students who major in environmental sciences.

During the conference, the doctoral students, as key players of the conference, not only undertook the main organizing work but also realized a new mode of “self-moderation, discussion, summary and commentary” which formed a high-level academic atmosphere for doctoral students. A variety forms of communication was adopted, including reports, poster displays, group sessions, seminars and academic salons. In order to improve its academic competence, each conference would invite scholarship winners to share their personal lessons and experience, including Qian Yi scholarship, national scholarship and comprehensive scholarship winner of Tsinghua University, all of such speeches are well received. During the academic exchange, conference organizers also hold exchange activities such as buffet reception, tour to Tsinghua University Art Museum, Olympic Park, Badachu and other attractions. These activities provide great opportunities for doctoral students to communicate, as well as improving their abilities to participate in academic exchange, which should be beneficial for expanding academic horizons, stimulating inspiration, inspiring scientific thinking and enhancing academic friendship.

The 12th “Environmental Conference for Doctoral Students” will be held on October 18th-21st, 2018, at Tsinghua University (Beijing, China), with a focus on water pollution control and resource recycling, air pollution control, solid waste pollution control and recycling, environmental economics and management and policy, environmental chemistry and materials, environmental ecology and health, energy and climate change, etc. There are sub-venues for new theories and new technology in environmental science and engineering, at the same time, with a venue set in English for better international communications. The conference will collect papers and compile them into “Proceedings of Environmental Conference for Doctoral Students”, and award several excellent posters, outstanding reports and papers. Finally, some of the best papers selected for conference proceedings will have the opportunity to be published in SCI journals such as Frontiers of Environmental Science & Engineering (IF = 1.716).

In order to inherit and carry forward the fine tradition of the former 11 conferences, the 12th “Environmental Conference for Doctoral Students” will continue to create a good atmosphere for academic exchanges for doctoral students and will continuously provide a platform for PhD students to exercise their abilities and enhance their friendship.

Selection

 

1、  Excellent Reports

1.1 Method of Evaluation

1, The judges are composed of conference participants and members of the organizing committee. According to the scoring standard for evaluation, the judges give scores in integer (full mark is 100); the average scores given by participants and organizing committee each account for 50% of the overall scores; the final score takes two digits after the decimal point;

2, The marking process will be carried out by the organizing committee at each sub-venue after all reports are completed, and will be submitted to the organizing committee for deliberation.

3, At the end of the conference, a maximum of 15 excellent oral reports will be awarded according to the final score.

4, The organizing committee reserves the right of final interpretation to the selection method.

1.2 Scoring Standards

1、Content (40 points)

Requirements: clearly organized, prominent theme, rich material, proper arrangement, accurate data and material.

Excellent: 40-31 points; good: 30-20 points; medium: less than 20 points.

 

2、Response to questions (20 points)

Requirements: grasp the focus, vivid and concise language, good explanation of the problem, easy to understand, no obvious mistakes.

Excellent: 20-16 points; good: 15-11 points; medium: less than 11 points.

 

3、Language expression (15 points)

Requirements: clear thinking, fluent language, easy to understand, rich and impressive, good use of words, calmand steady expressions.

Excellent: 15-12 points; good: 11-9 points; medium: less than 9 points.

 

4、Abstract (10 points)

Requirements: the structure is complete, the text is refined, the central point is prominent, the theme is distinct, the level is clear and the length is moderate.

Excellent: 10-9 points; good: 8-6 points; medium: less than 6 points

 

5、Powerpoint Slides (10 points)

Requirements: the slides are clear and understandable with illustrations, the color and design are decent, the transition between the slides is appropriate.

Excellent: 10-9 points; good: 8-6 points; medium: less than 6 points.

 

6、Time control (5 points)

The report time is 10-15 minutes with a 5-minute Q&A section. Those reports too long or too short (±5 minutes) would be likely to lose scores.

 

2、Excellent Poster

2.1 Selection method

1, The judges are composed of the conference participants and professional judges;

2, Each professional judge or participant has two votes for excellent posters. During the poster exchange session, the judges will put their own ballots into the boxes of selected posters. Each vote from professional judges is equivalent to 3 votes from the participants;

  1. The counting process will be carried out by the organizing committee at each sub-venue after all reports are completed, and will be submitted to the organizing committee for deliberation.

4, Based on the final votes, a maximum of 20 outstanding posters will be awarded;

5, The organizing committee reserves the right of final interpretation to the selection method.

 

2.2 Selection criteria

1、The content

Outstanding topics, rich materials, good arrangements, providing accurate materials.

2、Poster-making

Clear and well-illustrated picture, decent color and design.

3、Performance

In the poster exchange session, all poster authors could arrive at the conference venue and answer questions raised by other participants, with clear thinking, fluent language, easy explanation and impressive manners.

3、Comprehensive Excellence Award

Selection methods and standards

 

1, The judges are composed of members from the academic committee and organizing committee;

2, The scores are calculated according to oral reports, abstracts and scoringof papers, and poster votes, each accounting for 50%, 30% and 20% of the final score (the final score takes two digits after the decimal point;)

3, The scoring process will be carried out by the organizing committee at each sub-venue after all reports are completed, and will be submitted to the organizing committee for deliberation.

4, According to the overall scoring, a maximum of three comprehensive excellence awards will be given to participants;

5, The organizing committee reserves the right of final interpretation to the selection method.
