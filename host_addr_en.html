Beijing·Tsinghua University

Beijing is the capital of the People’s Republic of China and one of the most populous cities in the world. Beijing is the political, cultural, and educational center. It is a modernized historic city, now endowed with world-class convention centers, advanced facilities, beautiful hotels and resorts. It is home to the headquarters of most of China's largest state-owned companies.

 

Beijing has been the political center of the country for much of the past seven centuries. The city is renowned for its opulent palaces, temples, gardens, tombs, walls and gates, and its art treasures and universities have made it a center of culture and art in China.

 

There are several typical places of interests, such as The Forbidden City, The Great Wall, White Dragon Pond, Summer Palace, Bird’s Nest (National Stadium), Temple of Heaven, and National Theater.

 

As the imperial garden of the Emperor Kangxi (Yuyuan), Xichun Garden (predecessor of Tsinghua Garden) was originally built in 1767 (the 32nd year in Qianlong Emperor’s Reign), at around the middle of Qing Dynasty. The Xichun Garden and four other parks in the western suburbs of Beijing are called the “Five Gardens of Yuanming”.

 

During the Emperor Daoguang’s reign, the Xichun Garden was divided into the eastern and the western parts. Marked by the Gongzi Hall, the western part was called the Jinchun Garden, while the eastern part was still called the Xichun Garden. After Emperor Xianfeng was enthroned, the Xichun Garden was renamed the “Tsinghua Garden”. The existing threshold of Tsinghua Garden was given by Emperor Xianfeng of Qing Dynasty. The three Chinese characters “Tsinghua-yuan” was written by Yehenara Natong, one of the “three talents of the late Qing Dynasty.”

 

In 1860, the British and French troops burned the Jinchun Garden and Tsinghua Garden survived. In 1910, the United States built a “Tsinghua College” inside the campus with the Boxer Indemnity Funds. In 1913, Tsinghua College merged into the Jinchun Garden and became the Tsinghua University as we know it today.
